HUMAN RESOURCES AUDIT

With the changes in recent federal and state employment laws, it may be a good time to conduct an HR Audit. An HR audit will give you an objective look at your policies and HR practices and identify areas that may be improved or streamlined.

The Comprehensive HR Audit will typically cover the following areas:

1. Organizational Assessments: review of current company objectives to ensure alignment with HR strategy.
2. Recruitment: review of job descriptions, hiring and offer process, background screening, on-boarding, performance review, and termination process.
3. Personnel, benefit and I-9: file audit
4. Federal and State: employee posting compliance; workers compensation.
5. Policy & Employee: handbooks.
6. Total Rewards: compensation, benefits & payroll

Hire Ventures will be your Human Resource Partner in completing a comprehensive HR Audit.

Audit engagements are typically accomplished with an on site visit and tour of the facility, documentation review and final write up that will give you a written plan for your short and long term HR Strategy.

EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK CREATION OR REVISION

An employee handbook is one of the most important communication tools between you and your employees. It can provide guidance to managers and set expectations for employees and can also keep you legally sound if written well and updated as required.

We offer customized employee handbook creation as well as revision to your current employee handbook. We partner with you to learn your company’s values, culture, lingo and tenants and we weave those into your employee policies throughout the handbook.

Unsure if you need a Handbook Review? If it has been more than two years, it’s safe to say, yes! Time for a review and update:

1. Do you allow your employees to bring their own devices and use them on your network? If so, do you have a BYOD policy?
2. What are your social media guidelines? Do your employees understand them?
3. Do you have the required disclaimers or does your handbook create an implied employment contract?
4. Do you have an anti-discrimination, non-harassment and retaliation policy?
5. Have you updated your Military Leave policy, FMLA, Safety, Immigration compliance and EEO laws with the federal changes outlined in the past 18 months?

Are You A Start Up?

At this stage recruiting and human resource concerns are usually on the back burner as the focus is on raising capital.

After the founders have comprised their management team and are presenting their business plan to investors, a key component of the plan that will be reviewed is their ability to attract and ultimately hire the professional talent to ensure they can execute the plan presented.

Hire Ventures can help from these early stages and will work with the founders and management team to strategically design and execute the most effective recruiting and human resource strategy as well as provide appropriate HR support.
